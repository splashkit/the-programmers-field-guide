For this activity, try to join into a group of 3-4 people. The idea here is to fill in each other's knowledge gaps on a topic that frequently gets overlooked: the **compilation command**.

```sh title="For example..."
clang++ hello-world.cpp -o hello -l SplashKit -Wall
```

Things to consider before the activity:

- Have you really thought about what each section does? What does `-o` mean?
- Could you write it from scratch?
- Could there be other useful things you could _add_ to it?

You may have to do some extra Googling to figure this out. Work together and solidify your understanding!

## Group Activity

<Steps>
1. Gather around one of your group members' computers, and read over the compilation command below.

2. ##### Discuss the following with each other:
   1. _What_ is being compiled?

   3. _What_ will the resulting executable be called?
   4. Are there are pieces here we haven't learned about yet? What do they do?
